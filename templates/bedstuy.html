Information about Bed Stuy

Black Neighborhoods like Bed Stuy have been labeled as food deserts. That is, they have limited access to healthy and nutricious foods. Bed Stuy has a 57 bodega to supermarket ratio compared to the NYC average of 16. Bed Stuy always has a bodega of some sort in the corner of every street, however, the supermarkets are super hard to come by. After doing additional research and from my experience of going there, the supermarkets themselves are super crowded and unbearable to shop in. With high poverty neighborhoods set back by years of discrimination, Bed Stuy's food desert needs to be solved first, as the data proves it has the worst access to good food.

Solution: Start with Bed Stuy first. See what happens to the overall health and wellness of the people once there are supermarkets. The numbers will surely rise.
